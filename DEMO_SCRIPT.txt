[0:00 — HOOK]

Every day, brands receive thousands of social media mentions — complaints, praise, scam accusations, and everything in between. Responding too slowly costs you customers. Responding incorrectly costs you your reputation. I built the Social Media Monitor to solve exactly that.

[0:20 — WHAT IT IS]

Social Media Monitor is a multi-agent AI system built on Google's Agent Development Kit. It sits on the Agents for Business track and acts as a first responder for brand mentions — automatically analyzing sentiment, drafting replies aligned to your brand voice, logging incidents, and keeping a human in the loop for anything sensitive.

[0:40 — SHOW THE BANNER]

(show assets/cover_page_banner.png)

At a glance: Social Media Monitor is Secure, Automated, and Intelligent — three words that capture what this system delivers every time a post comes in.

[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]

(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)

Let me walk you through the flow.

Every incoming post first hits the Security Checkpoint node. This is where we scrub PII — emails and phone numbers — and check for prompt injection attacks. If something dangerous is detected, the flow immediately diverts to the Security Violation Handler and the process stops.

If the post is clean, it flows to the Orchestrator agent. The orchestrator is the brain. It calls the Sentiment Analyzer sub-agent, which classifies the post as positive, neutral, or negative and writes a summary. Then it calls the Response Drafter sub-agent, which fetches the brand voice guidelines and approved response templates from the MCP Server and drafts a reply.

If the sentiment is negative or the post triggered our profanity rule, the workflow pauses at the Review Gate. This is our Human-in-the-Loop node — a human operator sees the draft response and either approves it or provides feedback.

If feedback is given, the graph loops back to the orchestrator with the user's notes, and a revised draft is generated. Once the response is approved, it flows to the Final Output node where it's marked as published.

[3:00 — SECURITY & MCP]

The Security Checkpoint handles three controls: PII scrubbing with regex for emails and phone numbers, keyword-based prompt injection detection, and domain-specific flagging for scam-related language — all logging a structured JSON audit record every single time.

The MCP Server exposes three purpose-built tools: fetch_brand_guidelines for tone and length rules, search_response_templates for pre-approved reply snippets, and log_incident for CRM escalation tracking.

[3:30 — IMPACT / CLOSE]

This system benefits customer relations teams and brand managers who need to respond quickly without sacrificing quality or security. It can auto-handle 70 to 80 percent of routine positive and neutral posts, freeing human agents to focus on complex situations. For negative or sensitive posts, a human always has the final word before anything is published.

Social Media Monitor — built with Google ADK, designed for real business impact.
